SAP Knowledge Baseline: S/4HANA, SD, MM, and Joule
---
1. SAP — Company & Platform Overview
SAP SE is a German enterprise software company, one of the largest in the world by enterprise application market share.
Core product family: SAP S/4HANA (ERP), SAP BTP (Business Technology Platform — the cloud/integration/extension layer), SAP SuccessFactors (HR), SAP Ariba (procurement), SAP Customer Experience/CX (CRM), SAP Concur (travel/expense), SAP Fieldglass (contingent workforce).
SAP's underlying database technology is SAP HANA, an in-memory, columnar database that underpins S/4HANA's performance advantages over the older SAP ECC/R/3 systems.
SAP's implementation/consulting ecosystem includes certified partners (Accenture, Deloitte, Capgemini, etc.) and the SAP Community of independent practitioners.

2. SAP S/4HANA
What it is
S/4HANA ("SAP Business Suite 4 SAP HANA") is SAP's next-generation ERP suite, the successor to SAP ECC (ERP Central Component). It's built to run natively on the HANA in-memory database.
Key architectural differences from ECC
Simplified data model: Many ECC tables (e.g., separate tables for different financial document types) were consolidated. Example: the Universal Journal (table ACDOCA) merged FI and CO (financial and controlling) data into a single source of truth.
In-memory computing: Real-time analytics without needing separate aggregate tables or nightly batch jobs.
Fiori UI: Modern, role-based, responsive web UI replacing the old SAP GUI transaction-code-driven interface (though GUI/T-codes still exist underneath and are still used heavily).
Deployment options: On-premise, private cloud (RISE with SAP), and public cloud (SAP S/4HANA Cloud, Public Edition) — each with different customization flexibility and release cadence.
Embedded analytics: Real-time reporting embedded directly in transactional processes rather than requiring a separate BW/data warehouse extraction step for many use cases.
Modules (high level)
S/4HANA is organized into functional modules/lines of business, including:
FI/CO — Finance and Controlling
MM — Materials Management
SD — Sales and Distribution
PP — Production Planning
QM — Quality Management
PM — Plant Maintenance
EWM — Extended Warehouse Management
HCM (largely superseded by SuccessFactors for cloud customers)

3. SD — Sales and Distribution
Core purpose
Manages the order-to-cash process: everything from a customer inquiry/quote through delivery and billing.
Key process flow
Pre-sales activities — Inquiry, Quotation
Sales order creation — captures customer, material, quantity, pricing, delivery date
Availability check (ATP) — checks if stock can fulfill the order
Delivery processing — picking, packing, goods issue
Billing — invoice creation, which posts to FI
Credit management — checks customer credit exposure, often integrated with FI-CO
Key concepts/terms
Sales Organization, Distribution Channel, Division — the core organizational structure ("sales area") that every sales document is tied to.
Condition technique — SAP's rules-engine approach to pricing (condition types, condition records, pricing procedures).
Partner functions — roles like sold-to party, ship-to party, bill-to party, payer, which can all be different business partners on one order.
Copy control — rules governing how data flows/copies from one document type to another (e.g., quotation → sales order → delivery → billing document).
Common transaction codes historically include VA01 (create sales order), VA02/VA03 (change/display), VL01N (create delivery), VF01 (create billing document) — verify these are still current, as Fiori apps have replaced many of these for cloud customers.

4. MM — Materials Management
Core purpose
Manages the procure-to-pay process and inventory/materials handling.
Key process flow
Purchase requisition — internal request to buy
Purchase order (PO) — formal order to vendor
Goods receipt — receiving stock against the PO, updates inventory
Invoice verification (MIRO in classic terms) — matching vendor invoice to PO and goods receipt (3-way match)
Inventory management — stock types (unrestricted, quality inspection, blocked), movement types, valuation
Key concepts/terms
Material Master — the central record for every material, with different "views" (basic data, purchasing, MRP, accounting, etc.)
Vendor Master / Business Partner — in S/4HANA, vendors are modeled as Business Partners (a unification introduced compared to ECC's separate vendor master).
Material Ledger — S/4HANA's approach to material valuation, now mandatory (was optional in ECC), supports multiple currencies/valuations.
MRP (Material Requirements Planning) — plans what/when to procure or produce based on demand.
Movement types — three-digit codes defining the nature of a goods movement (e.g., goods receipt, goods issue, transfer).

5. SAP Joule
What it is
Joule is SAP's AI copilot/agent layer, embedded across the SAP product portfolio rather than being a single standalone chatbot.
Evolution (general trend, verify current state)
Started as a natural-language conversational assistant embedded in S/4HANA and SuccessFactors.
Evolved toward an agentic architecture — multiple specialized AI agents that can be orchestrated to complete multi-step business tasks, not just answer questions.
Joule Studio — a low-code/pro-code tool for building and customizing Joule agents/skills, including a VS Code extension and CLI for developers.
Increasing integration with SAP BTP as the technical backbone (Joule agents are built and deployed via BTP services).
Expansion into role-based assistants — routing users to specialized agents depending on their job function (finance, HR, procurement, etc.)
Cross-system orchestration — Joule is being positioned to coordinate not just within SAP but potentially with non-SAP enterprise systems via agent-to-agent protocols.
What I'm least reliable on for Joule specifically
Exact current number of agents/skills (this number has been actively growing — treat any specific count I give as a snapshot, not current truth)
Exact current UI/navigation paths
Pricing/licensing tiers
Exact product names for newer sub-features (these are rebranded/renamed relatively often)
