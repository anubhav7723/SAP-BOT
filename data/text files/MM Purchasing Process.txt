TOPIC: SAP MM - Purchasing Process (Procure-to-Pay)
MODULE: Materials Management (MM)
SCOPE: ECC 6.0 and S/4HANA

OVERVIEW
The purchasing process (Procure-to-Pay / P2P) covers the steps from
identifying a need to paying the vendor. Standard flow:
Purchase Requisition -> RFQ (optional) -> Purchase Order -> Goods Receipt ->
Invoice Receipt -> Payment.

DOCUMENT TYPES AND T-CODES
- Purchase Requisition (PR): internal request to purchase. Not legally
  binding, no vendor commitment yet.
  T-codes: ME51N (create), ME52N (change), ME53N (display).
- Request for Quotation (RFQ): request sent to vendor(s) for pricing.
  T-codes: ME41 (create), ME42 (change), ME43 (display).
- Quotation: vendor's response to an RFQ, maintained against the RFQ.
- Purchase Order (PO): legally binding document to a vendor.
  T-codes: ME21N (create), ME22N (change), ME23N (display).
  Older 3-screen transactions: ME21/ME22/ME23 (largely superseded by the "N" variants).
- Contract (Outline Agreement, value or quantity based): ME31K/ME32K/ME33K.
- Scheduling Agreement: long-term agreement with delivery schedules.
  T-codes: ME31L/ME32L/ME33L.
- Goods Receipt (GR): posting receipt of goods against a PO.
  T-code: MIGO (modern single-screen transaction covering GR, GI, transfer
  posting, etc.). Movement type 101 = GR into unrestricted stock.
- Invoice Receipt / Logistics Invoice Verification: T-code MIRO. This is
  where the "three-way match" (PO - Goods Receipt - Invoice) is checked.

THREE-WAY MATCH
The core control in Procure-to-Pay: the system compares PO quantity/price,
Goods Receipt quantity, and Invoice quantity/price. Discrepancies beyond
configured tolerances (T-code OMR6 for tolerance keys) cause the invoice to
be "blocked for payment" until resolved.

PURCHASING ORG STRUCTURE
- Purchasing Organization (EKORG): organizational unit responsible for procurement.
- Purchasing Group (EKGRP): buyer or buyer group responsible day-to-day.
- Plant (WERKS): where goods are received/consumed.
A Purchasing Organization can be assigned to one or more Company Codes/Plants
(centralized vs. plant-specific purchasing organization models).

INFO RECORD AND SOURCE LIST
- Purchasing Info Record (T-code ME11/ME12/ME13): stores vendor-material
  specific data - historical prices, vendor material number, planned delivery
  time. Tables: EINA (general/client level), EINE (purchasing-org level).
- Source List (T-code ME01): defines which vendor(s) are valid sources of
  supply for a material at a plant over a validity period, and whether a
  source is "fixed," "blocked," or usable for automatic PO creation via MRP.

PRICING IN PURCHASING
Purchasing documents use a condition technique similar to SD pricing
(different pricing procedure/condition tables, but conceptually parallel).
Common condition types: PB00 (gross price), PBXX (manual gross price), RA01,
RB00, FRA1 (freight), NAVS (non-deductible tax).

KEY BAPIS
- BAPI_PO_CREATE1: create a Purchase Order (see MM_TCodes_BAPIs_FMs.txt for
  parameter-level detail).
- BAPI_PO_CHANGE: change an existing PO.
- BAPI_PR_CREATE: create a Purchase Requisition.
- BAPI_GOODSMVT_CREATE: post a goods movement (GR, GI, transfer posting).
- BAPI_INCOMINGINVOICE_CREATE: post a vendor invoice (Logistics Invoice
  Verification equivalent of MIRO).

RELATED CONCEPTS
- Release Strategy: approval workflow for PRs/POs above certain value/other
  criteria thresholds, configured via classification (characteristics/
  release codes), T-codes ME54N (release PR), ME29N (release PO).
- Account Assignment Category: determines whether a PO item is stock
  (no account assignment) or consumed directly to a cost center/order/asset
  (account assignment categories K = cost center, F = order, A = asset, etc.).
