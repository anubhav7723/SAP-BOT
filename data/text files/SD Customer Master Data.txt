TOPIC: SAP SD - Customer Master Data
MODULE: Sales and Distribution (SD)
SCOPE: ECC 6.0 and S/4HANA

OVERVIEW
The Customer Master holds all data needed to process sales transactions with
a customer - address, sales terms, pricing-relevant data, shipping data,
and (in coordination with FI) accounting data. Like vendor/material master,
it is structured in levels: General Data, Company Code Data, and Sales Area
Data.

ORGANIZATIONAL LEVELS
- General Data: name, address, control data, unloading points, bank
  details - valid across the whole client. Table: KNA1.
- Company Code Data: reconciliation account, payment terms, dunning
  procedure. Table: KNB1.
- Sales Area Data: (Sales Org + Distribution Channel + Division specific)
  - order-related data, shipping data, billing data, partner functions.
  Table: KNVV.

S/4HANA CHANGE - BUSINESS PARTNER (BP)
As with vendors, S/4HANA moves customer master maintenance to the Business
Partner model. Customers are created/maintained via T-code BP with a
"Customer" role, and the system synchronizes data to the classic KNA1/KNB1/
KNVV tables via the Customer/Vendor Integration (CVI) layer. In many S/4HANA
releases the classic T-codes (VD01/VD02/XD01/XD02) are blocked/hidden by
default, with BP as the mandated entry point - exact enforcement is
release/config dependent.

KEY TABLES
- KNA1: Customer master (general data) - KUNNR (Customer Number), NAME1,
  LAND1, SPRAS.
- KNB1: Customer master (company code data) - KUNNR, BUKRS, AKONT
  (reconciliation account), ZTERM.
- KNVV: Customer master (sales area data) - KUNNR, VKORG, VTWEG, SPART,
  KDGRP (Customer Group), WAERS (currency), VSBED (shipping conditions),
  INCO1/INCO2 (Incoterms).
- KNVP: Customer partner functions per sales area (which partner - sold-to,
  ship-to, bill-to, payer - is assigned).
- KNVI: Customer tax classification.
- KNVK: Contact persons.

KEY FIELDS
- KUNNR: Customer account number.
- VKORG / VTWEG / SPART: together define a "Sales Area," the key
  organizational context for all sales-relevant customer and material data.

PARTNER FUNCTIONS (customer side)
- SP (Sold-To Party): the customer who places the order.
- SH (Ship-To Party): where goods are delivered - can differ from sold-to.
- BP (Bill-To Party): who receives the invoice.
- PY (Payer): who is responsible for payment.
A single sales order can involve up to four different customer numbers via
these roles; they default from the customer master's partner determination
but can be overridden per document. (Note: "BP" here is the partner function
code for Bill-to Party, not to be confused with T-code BP for Business
Partner maintenance - same two letters, different meaning/context.)

RELATED CONCEPTS
- Customer Account Group: controls number range and field selection
  (e.g., distinguishing sold-to vs. one-time customer).
- Customer-Material Info Record (table KNMT): customer-specific material
  data (customer's own material number, delivery tolerances) - analogous to
  the Purchasing Info Record on the procurement side.
- Credit Management: in ECC, classic FI-AR credit management (tables KNKA/
  KNKK); in S/4HANA, credit management is handled by FSCM Credit Management
  (UKM* tables), a more significant architectural difference - verify which
  model a given S/4HANA system uses.
