TOPIC: SAP SD - Delivery Process (Outbound Logistics)
MODULE: Sales and Distribution (SD) / Logistics Execution (LE)
SCOPE: ECC 6.0 and S/4HANA

OVERVIEW
The delivery process moves goods from the warehouse to the customer,
covering delivery creation, picking, packing, and Post Goods Issue (PGI).
It sits between the Sales Order and the Billing Document in the standard
document flow.

KEY STEPS AND T-CODES
- Create Outbound Delivery: T-code VL01N (create with reference to a sales
  order), VL02N (change), VL03N (display). Deliveries can also be created
  in bulk via VL10A / VL10C (collective delivery creation worklists).
- Picking: confirming the quantity taken from a storage location/bin, either
  directly on the delivery (Picking tab) or via Warehouse Management/
  Extended Warehouse Management if active.
- Packing: assigning delivery items to handling units (T-code VL01N/VL02N
  packing tab, or HU02).
- Post Goods Issue (PGI): the delivery is confirmed as physically shipped;
  this reduces inventory and creates the accounting-relevant goods movement
  (movement type 601 for standard sales delivery). PGI can be executed from
  VL02N or in mass via VL06G / a dedicated PGI worklist.

DELIVERY DOCUMENT STRUCTURE
- Header: ship-to party, shipping point, route, planned/actual goods issue
  date.
- Item: material, delivery quantity, batch (if applicable), reference to
  the originating sales order item.

KEY ORGANIZATIONAL ELEMENTS
- Shipping Point: the physical location (loading dock/area) responsible for
  processing a delivery - determined from plant + shipping condition +
  loading group.
- Route: determines the transportation path/mode and transit time,
  influences delivery scheduling.

KEY TABLES
- LIKP: Delivery Header - VBELN (delivery number), LFART (delivery type),
  KUNNR (ship-to), WADAT_IST (actual goods issue date), ROUTE.
- LIPS: Delivery Item - VBELN, POSNR, MATNR, LFIMG (delivery quantity),
  VGBEL/VGPOS (reference sales document/item), WERKS, LGORT.
- VBFA: Document Flow table - links delivery back to sales order and
  forward to billing document (see SD_MM_Document_Flow.txt for detail).
- VBUK / VBUP: overall and item-level status also reflect delivery status
  (e.g., WBSTK = goods movement status) on the referenced sales order.

KEY BAPIs
- BAPI_OUTB_DELIVERY_CREATE_MULT / BAPI_OUTB_DELIVERY_CREATE_SLS: create
  outbound delivery(ies) with reference to sales orders (naming and exact
  availability differs by release; there are several delivery-creation BAPI
  variants historically, so verify which one is current for the target
  release before relying on a specific name).
- BAPI_OUTB_DELIVERY_CHANGE: change an outbound delivery (e.g., update
  picked quantities).
- BAPI_OUTB_DELIVERY_CONFIRM_DEC: confirm/post goods issue for an outbound
  delivery (PGI).

SCHEDULING CONCEPTS
Delivery scheduling works backward/forward from the requested delivery date
using lead times: transportation lead time, loading time, pick/pack time,
and transit time, to determine material availability date, transportation
planning date, loading date, and goods issue date.

RELATED CONCEPTS
- Delivery Split: a single sales order can result in multiple deliveries
  (e.g., split by shipping point, route, or partial availability); conversely
  delivery combination can merge multiple orders into one delivery if
  header data matches, depending on configuration.
- Batch determination can occur during delivery creation for batch-managed
  materials.
