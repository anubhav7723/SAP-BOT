TOPIC: SAP MM - Inventory Management (Goods Movements)
MODULE: Materials Management (MM)
SCOPE: ECC 6.0 and S/4HANA

OVERVIEW
Inventory Management (IM) tracks physical and value movements of stock:
goods receipts, goods issues, transfer postings, and physical inventory.
Every movement is recorded with a Movement Type that determines the
accounting/stock impact.

KEY TRANSACTION: MIGO
MIGO is the modern single-screen transaction used for most goods movements
(Goods Receipt, Goods Issue, Transfer Posting, Display, Cancellation). It
replaced older separate transactions like MB01 (GR), MB1A (GI), MB1B
(transfer posting) for day-to-day use, though several of the older MB*
transactions still exist and are used in some processes/reports.

COMMON MOVEMENT TYPES (Bewegungsart)
- 101: Goods Receipt for Purchase Order into unrestricted-use stock.
- 102: Reversal of 101 (GR reversal).
- 201: Goods Issue to a Cost Center.
- 261: Goods Issue for a Production/Process Order (consumption).
- 301: Transfer Posting, plant to plant (in one step).
- 311: Transfer Posting, storage location to storage location (same plant).
- 601: Goods Issue for Delivery (in SD, triggered from outbound delivery / PGI).
- 641: Goods Issue for Stock Transport Order (STO) delivery.
- 701/702: Physical inventory - stock gain/loss postings.
Movement types are configurable; the above are the SAP-standard defaults
most commonly seen.

STOCK TYPES
- Unrestricted-use stock: freely usable stock, available for consumption/sale.
- Quality inspection stock: goods received but pending QM inspection.
- Blocked stock: not usable (e.g., rejected, damaged).
- Stock in transit: for stock transport orders between plants.

KEY TABLES
- MSEG: Material Document Item - the line-item detail of every goods movement
  (MATNR, WERKS, LGORT, MENGE, BWART, MBLNR, MJAHR).
- MKPF: Material Document Header - MBLNR, MJAHR, BUDAT, CPUDT (posting/entry date).
- MARD: Storage location stock quantities (updated by movements) - LABST
  (unrestricted), INSME (quality insp.), SPEME (blocked).
- MCHB: Batch stock (if batch management active).

PHYSICAL INVENTORY
Cyclical or periodic stock counting process to reconcile system stock with
physical stock. T-codes: MI01 (create physical inventory document), MI04
(enter count), MI07 (post difference). Related tables: ISEG (physical
inventory document items).

KEY BAPI/FM
- BAPI_GOODSMVT_CREATE: the standard BAPI for posting goods movements
  programmatically. It requires a GOODSMVT_HEADER (posting date, document
  date, movement reference) and a GOODSMVT_ITEM table (material, plant,
  storage location, movement type, quantity). The GOODSMVT_CODE parameter
  determines the movement's business context (e.g., "01" for goods receipt
  for purchase order, "02" for goods issue, etc.) - exact code list is
  release-dependent; verify in the system's BAPI documentation (SE37) if the
  exact code value matters for your scenario.

RELATED CONCEPTS
- Valuation is tightly coupled to Inventory Management: every quantity-
  relevant goods movement typically also creates a value posting in FI/CO
  based on the material's price control (Standard 'S' or Moving Average 'V',
  see MBEW-VPRSV).
- Stock Overview: T-code MMBE, gives a consolidated view of stock across
  plants/storage locations/stock types for a material.
